Service detail

Seasonal cleanup for yards that need a reset before maintenance.

Leaves, storm debris, old mulch, bed clutter, overgrowth, and curbline buildup can hide the shape of a yard. Cleanup brings the property back to a workable baseline.

Service depth

Cleanup is the reset before everything else.

When leaves, branches, and bed debris accumulate, the yard stops being readable. Cleanup reveals turf, hardscape, drainage, bed edges, and hazards so maintenance can be planned correctly.

What homeowners usually need

A yard that is safe to walk, easier to mow, cleaner from the street, and ready for a seasonal change, listing, event, or recurring maintenance.

The scope depends heavily on volume and disposal, not just yard size.

What providers need to know

Leaf depth, wet debris, branch size, bagging rules, pickup location, trailer access, fragile beds, lighting wires, drains, and priority areas.

A cleanup can be quick or equipment-heavy depending on these details.

What it covers

Leaf removal, branch and debris collection, bed cleanup, light shrub trimming, curbline clearing, mulch prep, and bagging or haul-away coordination.

When users need it

Spring opening, fall leaf load, storm cleanup, pre-event preparation, move-in or move-out yard resets, and properties paused between maintenance cycles.

Details that matter

Debris volume, municipal pickup rules, access for bags or trailers, wet leaves, hidden obstacles, fragile plantings, and areas that should not be disturbed.

Typical approach

Providers confirm volume and disposal path, clear priority zones, reset bed and hardscape edges, and identify whether recurring maintenance should follow.

Visual reset

Cleanup makes the property legible again.

Leaves and debris hide edges, drainage paths, lawn condition, bed shape, and trip hazards. A cleanup request should show volume, access, and priority zones before a provider confirms disposal options.

Best photos to send

Front yard, backyard, curbline, bed edges, largest leaf piles, branches, access route, and any municipal pickup area if bags or piles must be placed curbside.

Practical questions

Common cleanup details.

Does cleanup include haul-away?

Only if a provider confirms it. Some services bag for municipal pickup, others can quote haul-away separately.

Why do wet leaves change pricing?

Wet leaves are heavier, slower to move, harder on equipment, and may require different handling.

Can cleanup and mowing happen together?

Often yes, but heavy debris usually needs to be cleared first so mowing is safe and effective.

Methods and prep

Small prep choices can shorten the visit.

Provider-side checks

  • Confirm disposal or curb-placement requirements
  • Separate leaves, branches, and general yard debris where needed
  • Protect beds, lighting, irrigation heads, and fragile plants
  • Identify whether mowing or turf work should follow

Homeowner-side prep

  • Move furniture, toys, hoses, and planters if possible
  • Share pickup rules, bag limits, or HOA requirements
  • Flag hidden obstacles, drains, lighting, and low-voltage wires
  • Mark priority zones if the entire yard cannot be completed at once
